AccelerateIBD International Business Development

Our Mission

To identify, capture and build the international potential of your business.

To guide the strategic consideration of why and how to best build your international presence.

Our Services

Scope

Whether embarking on overseas expansion, wanting to move into additional markets or needing to restructure under-performing areas of an existing international presence, we bring expertise and reinforce your resources. We guide your team to focus on the fundamentals of extending your reach and diagnose gaps in competencies and capacity to attain your objectives. Our aim is to assist you to move smarter.

Resource

We are accomplished in market analysis, partner selection, contract agreements, establishing distribution channels, creating actionable plans and networking for sustained, profitable expansion. We will guide the transition to culturally adapted marketing programs and the development of an effective commercial approach.

Our experience is worldwide, across most classes of trade, especially in the consumer and industrial B to B sectors. It encompasses all disciplines for advancing business and the assessment of market entry options, including both legal and pricing structures.

Results

We take the long term view of exporting: to prioritize viable opportunities, finding or supporting overseas partner's that respect the value of collaboration in representing your brand, while strengthening your organizational capacity to maintain vibrant international operations and sales.

Additional service

We also promote the entry and growth of new enterprise into the US market. We can assist in optimizing your supply chain and presenting alternatives for overseas manufacturing and licensing.

who we Are

Tony Patterson

From the European trading HQ of Mitsubishi Corporation in London covering Europe, Middle East, Africa and Japan, he joined Business International in Rome (management consulting, now part of the Economist Group) advising major corporations on trading, investment conditions, regulations and strategies across Africa and Europe.

Moved into International Sales and Marketing with Mitsubishi Corporation European trading HQ in London, engaged in market and sector research, project initiation, financing and execution covering Europe, Middle East, Africa and Japan. Joined Business International in Rome (management consulting, merged into the Economist Group) advising major corporations on trading, investment conditions, regulations and strategies across Africa and Europe.

This was followed by corporate, marketing and senior regional management roles with Philip Morris International based in Switzerland, Nigeria, Ivory Coast, Senegal, Egypt and Dubai.

Relocated to head up the UK publicly listed Adam & Harvey Group, engaged in diversified trading and distribution, with operations in Asia, Africa, Europe and the US covering transport spare parts, bus assembly, coach manufacture, textiles, agricultural machinery and electric equipment, timber, steel distribution and general trading.

In 2007 moved to USA as COO of premium beauty brand with US manufacturing and multi-channel sales in US and international markets through distributors. Then CEO at premium gift and beauty brand Crabtree & Evelyn in the US, including the global manufacturing facility, ecommerce fulfillment and marketing functions, with some 200 suppliers, exported to all regions of the world and sold through a mix of branded stores, distributors and third party channels.

Latterly independent business consultant active between Europe and the US. Diverse experience across many cultures and go-to-market strategies, branded products, scaling teams and companies, nurturing partners and raising finance.

Tony is a Fellow of the UK Institute of Directors and lives between the US and Europe. Grew up in England, Scotland, Malta, Cyprus, Gibraltar, and Libya, graduated in Economics in London.

Patricio Forno

Mr. Forno studied Industrial Engineering at the Universidad de Chile and attended Business School at the Universidad Diego Portales, both in Santiago, Chile, his native country.

Patricio’s Chilean professional life included a successful entrepreneurial venture selling fire prevention equipment, executive management of one of the country’s biggest wallcovering importers/distributors, and running the operations of a leading construction wholesale business and retail chain store. While guiding in Patagonia, he met his American wife. After naturalizing to the US, he directed the sales and operations of a home furnishings manufacturer and then ran his own financial trading enterprise for 5 years. In 2011, he joined a leading private US manufacturer, marketer and distributor of mineral cosmetics with multichannel distribution in the US and in 29 international markets. Tasked with the challenge of developing additional markets, the share of international sales grew from 20% to 30%, expanding, between 2011 and 2015, to 50 international markets. This sustained export sales growth earned the company the 2016 US Presidential “E” Award, the highest recognition for significant contribution to US exports.

A member of the District Export Council of Massachusetts (DEC), appointed under the U.S. Secretary of Commerce, Patricio lives in Salisbury, CT. He enjoys climbing mountains, paragliding and riding his motorcycle with his wife, Cathy.
